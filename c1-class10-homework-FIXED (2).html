1. Reading

Immigration and the American Story

Immigration has shaped the United States throughout its history. Waves of newcomers have arrived from Europe, Asia, Latin America, Africa, and beyond, each group bringing languages, customs, foods, and skills that became part of American culture.

The journey is rarely easy. Immigrants often face language barriers, unfamiliar systems, and the challenge of rebuilding professional credentials. Many work in industries that depend on their labor, from agriculture and construction to healthcare and technology.

Over time, immigrant communities have made lasting contributions. They have founded businesses, created art, advanced science, and strengthened neighborhoods. The story of immigration is also a story of resilience, adaptation, and belonging.
